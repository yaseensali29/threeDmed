Nucleus
The nucleus is the control center of the neuron. It contains the cell's DNA and regulates gene expression, protein synthesis, and overall cellular function. It coordinates the neuron's activities and maintains its genetic information.
Pulses
Electrical impulses (action potentials) that travel along axons, transmitting signals between neurons. These pulses represent the rapid electrical signals that enable neural communication.
Synapse Bursts
Brief flashes of activity at synapses where neurotransmitters are released. These bursts represent the moment of communication between neurons, when signals are transmitted across the synaptic gap.
